#linear-algebra 

(of a [[vector space]]) consider vector spaces W and V, such that $W \subset V$, and a mapping
$$T:V \rightarrow V$$

If $\vec{w} \in W$ implies $T(\vec{w}) \in W$, then W is an invariant subspace of V for T, or *T-invariant*.
